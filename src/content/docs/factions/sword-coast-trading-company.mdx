The **Sword Coast Trading Company** is the chronicle's engine of empire — "the largest imperial
charter company in the Western sea and beyond," which grew from a Baldur's Gate shipping concern
into an interplanar power that "altered the face of civilization." Its first flagship was the
slaver privateer *Evening Star*; its fortune was built on the [moonstone](/concepts/moonstone/) of
[Motherstone](/locations/motherstone/) and the secrets of the **Infernal Machines**.

## A company of one face

<Oblivia label="Who really runs the Company">
The Company is run by **Dox** — a changeling collective whose members each wear the exact face of
chairman [Wainwright St. Cloud](/characters/wainwright-st-cloud/). Inside St. Cloud Tower in
[Sigil](/locations/), "almost all the staff are dressed and look exactly like Wainwright St.
Cloud." Its true chief executive is **Dox Prime**, the only one who meets the Boss in person; every
department — shipping, mining, manufacturing, security, R&D, and the shadowy **Special Projects** —
is headed by a numbered Dox. The Company is, functionally, one immortal man wearing a thousand
masks.
</Oblivia>

## What it built

From the Deepmaker at Motherstone it mass-produced the **Warforged** war-constructs and the
**Arcane Core** that drives its airships; from the Netherese machines it derived consumer devices
wired to the [Obliviator](/items/the-obliviator/)'s leylines, able to "broadcast any message…
into the minds of nearby sentient beings." Its Special Projects hunts the buried **Mammon Machine**
and the reborn Infantas. After two failed Battles of Motherstone and the breaking of its Sigil hub,
the Company is "crippled and scattered, leaving assets and employees up for grabs."

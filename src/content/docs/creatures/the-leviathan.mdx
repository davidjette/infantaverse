The **Leviathan** — **Cthulhu**, **Mammon**, **Zoth** — is the chronicle's final antagonist: a
thousand-foot-tall Great Old One folded in [the Egg](/locations/), and the doom every age has been
building toward. It is the thing that has been coming "since the first star went dark."

## The blind devourer

<Oblivia label="What the Leviathan is — June's revelation">
At the end of time, the goddess June names the truth. The gods of the realm are "the split soul of
the Star Child, whose name was **Zoth-Ommog**," who fled their father across all of time:

> My father, Cthulhu, who you call Mammon, or Leviathan, **is blind. He remembers all but can see
> none.** He moves by omniscience but he cannot see inside this delicate place we had achieved… He
> consumes all worlds in his pursuit of us. Once the evening sky was bright with stars, but he
> swallowed nearly every one.

He devours by *remembering* — which is why his children "created Time itself, to hide those things
from him that happen not all at once." He cannot enter that hiding place; "he can only corrupt it,
with fear… He always finds a vessel, one who will Travel on his behalf" — the **Traveler**, the
role [Wainwright St. Cloud](/characters/wainwright-st-cloud/) plays.
</Oblivia>

## The Call, and the Apotheosis

The Leviathan's chant — *Ph'nglui mglw'nafh Cthulhu R'lyeh wgah'nagl fhtagn* — presses itself into
every mind near him, and his **Call** reaches sensitives like [Calix](/characters/calix/) across
the world. His servant St. Cloud manipulates the Netherese into the **Apotheosis of Man** — marrying
the [Mammon Machine](/items/) to the Egg — believing they will make a "New Star," when in truth it
would channel the souls of the dead into the Egg until "Cthulhu remerges with the remnants of Zoth,
and Time and Consciousness itself give way to total oblivion."

## The Egg

He cannot be reached except through the Crack at [Motherstone](/locations/motherstone/), into
[the Egg](/locations/) where geometry confounds and a sea of acid dissolves all but him. He is
fought in pieces — Head, Claws, Tentacles, Wings — each regenerating; he dies only when his head is
destroyed and then **touched with an [Obliviator](/items/the-obliviator/)**. That act — the
[End of the Infanta](/events/end-of-the-infanta/) — unwrites him, and the heroes with him.

Calix is the architect of the **Forgetting** — the elf-mage who buried a kingdom, and his own
mind, beneath the memory of the world. He is the chronicle's deepest villain and, by its end, one
of its strangest allies.

## Biography

Calix was the brother of **Eldamir**, founder of [House Gallidann](/factions/), in the age the
elves call the Heavenfall. Where Eldamir built a house, Calix "moved into solitary study of magic
on a nearby archipelago." When a human child showed mastery of elemental magic — the first
**Infanta**, [Elistrada](/characters/) — "Calix demanded the child as his pupil [but] Eldamir
refused and they slay each other in battle." But Calix had refused the grave: he became a
*baelnorn*, an elven lich, "stored parts of his soul throughout the smaller islands in hopes to
extend his life even beyond that of an elf," and allied with **Queen Maab**, goddess of air and
darkness, to seize the island's power again and again.

## The Betrayal

His true crime was not ambition but **the Forgetting**, recorded in [Mara Mageblood's
Journal](/items/) only in a lullaby — *"never sing this song again of Calix the Betrayer"* — and
in the verse of an ancient drama.

<Oblivia label="Calix's gambit — the buried throne">
At the height of Motherstone, Calix buried his own terminal-throne to *replace the
[Lexicon](/items/the-lexicon/) with himself* — to dissolve his mind entirely into the great
crystal and become one with the Motherstones, using the Lexicon and the
[Obliviator](/items/the-obliviator/) to gather all the knowledge of the world. He did it to
outlast the one thing he fears:

> My sin is the Forgetting.
> Time is the only recompense.
> If I live until I know myself again
> I will live forever.

He has forgotten *himself* along with everything else — "he seeks, but knows not what for." Mara
read the same truth: *"a buried throne for a living god, for even he fears the [Leviathan](/creatures/the-leviathan/)."*
</Oblivia>

## Death, and after

By the close of [Arc II — Motherstone](/events/motherstone/), Calix is destroyed — but not before
nearly crowning the reborn Infanta on the poisoned throne, undone only by the sound of
Brightmantle's music box. By the [Age of the Infanta](/events/age-of-the-infanta/) he is dead, and
the Company spends years failing to "unlock the Calix puzzle" in the broken Lexicon. Yet his sigil
is found emblazoned through the Old Man's House in [Sigil](/locations/), and in the rewritten past
of the [Time of Troubles](/events/time-of-troubles/) a haggard, undead **Masked Prophet** matching
his description guides the elves toward ruin. By the end he hears the **Call of Cthulhu** himself,
and turns — using his visions to expose how [Wainwright St. Cloud](/characters/wainwright-st-cloud/)
deceived the Netherese queens into serving the Leviathan.

## Relationships

Brother of **Eldamir**; rival and would-be master of the first Infanta **Elistrada**; servant of
**Queen Maab**; and the unwilling author of the absence that this whole record exists to recover.

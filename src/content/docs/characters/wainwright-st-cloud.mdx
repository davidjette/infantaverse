**Wainwright St. Cloud** is the chronicle's great manipulator: chairman of the [Sword Coast
Trading Company](/factions/sword-coast-trading-company/), "High Counselor to the Emperor… and
possibly the most powerful man in the world. His motivations are mystery, his methods are a maze."
Brother of the slaver captain Ysmir and the imperial commander Carlysle, he outlived them both —
because he is far older than any of them.

## The Company

From a Baldur's Gate shipping concern, St. Cloud built an interplanar empire on the moonstone of
[Motherstone](/locations/motherstone/), centered on the city of [Sigil](/locations/). He runs it
through **Dox** — a changeling collective whose members each wear his exact face — and from behind a
portrait in his tower lies his true lair, the **Old Man's House**, a palace older than memory hung
with the Calix sigil and with paintings of the heroes "over and over again, in slightly different
versions."

## What he is

<Oblivia label="St. Cloud is Alphalpha Beginnigin">
St. Cloud's own oldest memo, claiming the estate of a dead man named **Alphalpha Beginnigin**,
gives him away: *"We can sign his name for godsake! We ARE. HIM."* The **Lady of Pain**, ruler of
Sigil, confirms it — "Alphalpha Beginnigin and Wainwright St. Cloud are one [and] the same… He
destroyed me and let Asmodeus take the land of the dead." He is an immortal who has lived through
fall after fall, and who "caused both the First and Second Fall."
</Oblivia>

He sees himself as the axis of history: *"I am the progenitor of countless epochs of bountiful life
and history. I am its witness, I am its doctor, I am its author, and I am its death. I am the
protagonist of reality, and you are my foil."* And he is aware he moves inside a bent timeline —
taunting the heroes that "a little goddess of time has been altering the flow of time for me…
locking me in some kind of relative loop."

## His design

St. Cloud's every scheme — the overridden Lexicon, the broadcast rings, the search for the buried
**Mammon Machine** — serves one end: to wake [the Leviathan](/creatures/the-leviathan/). As his
agent Dox Prime confesses, "Leviathan is coming. May it know that I helped it come to be." He is
the **Traveler** the god always finds — the vessel through whom Cthulhu reaches for the world. He
flees toward the Mammon Machine at the close of [Arc IV](/events/the-infernal-machines/), and the
heroes are told their purpose is "to destroy the Traveler."

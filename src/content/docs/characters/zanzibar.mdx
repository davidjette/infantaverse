**Zanzibar** is the hero who arrives with the new age: a prince of Cormyr and a man of faith, drawn
to [Motherstone](/locations/motherstone/) by the same dread that grips every priest in
[Arc III](/events/age-of-the-infanta/) — that the holy magic of the gods is quietly failing.

## The envoy

As the old gods begin incarnating into mortal children and priests across Faerûn fret over "a second
Time of Troubles," it is this fear "that draws new champions to Motherstone — among them
**Zanzibar**, a prince of Cormyr dispatched by the high priest of Punis to ask
[Brother Magnus](/characters/brother-magnus/) about his vision and the fading of the gods." He comes
as a questioner and stays as a hero.

## Una

Zanzibar's thread becomes the arc's quiet heart. In **Suzail**, the City of Song, during the ten-day
winter festival of **Candlenights**, "Zanzibar carries home a girl named **Una** — drowned and
resurrected… who may or may not be an [Infanta](/concepts/the-infanta/) herself." Her grief, and the
War Wizards' scheme to seize her and "use her to conduct a world war," pull him straight into the
chronicle's race for the reborn gods.

**Motherstone** was once the capital of the world — a crystal city grown around the largest
[moonstone](/concepts/moonstone/) deposit on the plane, hidden in a crater lake atop Snowy Mountain
on [Snowdown](/locations/). The heroes break into it in [Arc II](/events/motherstone/):

> A magnificent outgrowth of blue moonstone crystals ten stories tall, surrounded by an elven city,
> encircled by the mountain itself… A tremendous, eerily motionless, bronze ring the size of a
> sailing ship seems fixed above the largest moonstone, with an equally motionless orb floating in
> its center.

That orb is the [Obliviator](/items/the-obliviator/); the crystal's heart holds the
[Lexicon](/items/the-lexicon/). Together they ran the city — and together they erased it.

## Its peoples

Two peoples inherit the ruin: the **[Mundi](/factions/)**, white moon-elves who live in the great
oaks and revere the crystals under their king Chad; and the **[Drek](/factions/)**, a
spider-worshipping folk who carved the old mines and now drown in a rising flood in their slum, the
Drembles. Both are heirs of a betrayal neither can remember.

## The Forgetting

<Oblivia label="Why no one remembered Motherstone">
Motherstone "went from the capital of the world to entirely forgotten in a day." The
[Obliviator](/items/the-obliviator/), bound up with [Calix](/characters/calix/)'s buried mind,
erased the city and its dwarf-king builder from the memory of everyone who ever lived. What
survived was a hole — ledgers that skip, ruins no one can place, the explorer
[Mara Mageblood](/characters/) who read of it and went half-blind. It is the founding instance of
the **Oblivia**.
</Oblivia>

## A wound in the world

Beneath the city lies **the Crack** — the slice the dead call it — the only known approach to
**[the Egg](/locations/)**, the prison-womb of the sleeping god. Motherstone is fought over twice
by the [Company](/factions/sword-coast-trading-company/): the First and Second Battles of
Motherstone bracket the [Age of the Infanta](/events/age-of-the-infanta/). When the
[Lexicon](/items/the-lexicon/) breaks, the city — dependent on its magic for everything from
teaching children to moving its own architecture — falls into ruin and class war, "ripping at the
seams."

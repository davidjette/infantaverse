**Sigil**, the **City of Doors**, is "the most central city on the most central point of all of
existence" — a city of spires "perched atop an impossibly tall formation," where "portals to other
worlds and even the depths of hell are tucked into windows, doorways, potholes, and street signs.
Each of these portals requires a key or phrase to activate them, and these secrets are the currency
of this city." It is ruled by the **Lady of Pain** — who proves to be the sister of
[Marcus](/characters/marcus/), the Raven Queen.

## A drowning refuge

As the gods vanish into mortal [Infantas](/concepts/the-infanta/), the outer planes collapse, and
their angels and demons flood into Sigil. The cube-shaped **Modrons** check every refugee's papers
and herd the rest back to dying demiplanes — until "Port Somewhere is now an industrial interplanar
deportation machine."

## The Company's heart

Sigil is the true seat of the [Sword Coast Trading Company](/factions/sword-coast-trading-company/).
**St. Cloud Tower** dominates the main port, staffed entirely by the **Dox** collective; behind a
portrait in its executive suite lies the **Old Man's House**, the millennia-old palace of
[Wainwright St. Cloud](/characters/wainwright-st-cloud/), emblazoned throughout with the sigil of
[Calix](/characters/calix/). The heroes break the Company's interplanar empire here in
[Arc IV](/events/the-infernal-machines/), and the Lady of Pain burns the Old Man's House to ash.

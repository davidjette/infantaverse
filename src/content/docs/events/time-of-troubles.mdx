The sixth arc unfolds in a past that has been *edited*. Scattered through time after Starfall, the
heroes reunite in a mythic age of falling empires and reborn gods — and find that they can change
it. Here, gathering the legends of Camelot and Avalon and the elven founders against the rising
power of Netheril, the chronicle's deepest history finally surfaces, spoken aloud by the living
moonblades who lived it. One session doc opens with the whole arc's thesis, attributed to a very
familiar archmage:

> The distinction between the past, present and future is only a stubbornly persistent illusion.
> — Archmage Z-Alberton Einsteinianion

## A wish in time

Cornered by the forces of **Talona**, **Kara** spends a Wish — that they *had* moved the Moon in
the past — and the act rewrites reality around them. The citadel transforms; the walls fill with
**Pim's** chronicle of a history in which the deflection succeeded; the God of Medicine becomes an
ally instead of an enemy. In a world remade by a single sentence, the party resolves to gather
allies for the war to come, beginning with **Brightmantle** — and are drawn into the oldest legend
of the age.

## Camelot

The mercy-and-justice myth of the Infantaverse is Arthurian, and tragic. **Nimue** (Vivien), one
of the five Banefae and founder of **Avalon**, foresaw the Time of Troubles and sought a human
king to raise a rival to Netheril and "create a culture of peace and justice among the men of
Earth." She gave **Uther Els** her sword **Excalibur** — also called **Ironbane** — and set her
lover **Merlin** to watch his line. Uther's treachery broke the gift: Merlin stole away the child,
Uther drove Excalibur into the stone, and **Arthur Els** grew to take up the legend.

But treachery ran deeper. **Morgana La Fay** trapped Merlin in an oak, stole his power, and bore an
incestuous son in a rite that bound the **God of Mercy and Judgment** into him: **Mordred**, who
would become **the Jesu, the [Infanta](/concepts/the-infanta/) of Judgment**. With a false Grail-promise, Morgana and Mordred
allied with the Infanta-less dwarves of **Mithral Hall**, preaching that all gods are one —
Moradin — and that Jesu was his son come "to bring deliverance to believers and hellfire for
heretics." The party's charge is plain: destroy Mordred and La Fay, free Merlin, and ally with the
elves of Avalon. Along the way they take in a young would-be knight, **Parsifal**.

<Oblivia label="The making of Merlin, and the corruption of Queen Maab">
In Avalon, learning the **Charm of Making** to unbind Merlin, Kara uncovers the fae history behind
Arc I's dark goddess. Merlin himself was engineered: the elves of the Summer Court, "desperate to
break their curse of infertility and isolation from their divine aspect of the moon," bargained
with **Asmodeus** to sire a child who "could look backward into time" — a wizard with "one foot in
the land of dreams."

And **Queen Maab** — the Queen of Air and Darkness, Calix's dark patron — was made by a stone:

> Legend has it that in the time before history began, dwarven miners had unearthed a ten-faceted
> black gemstone of dark beauty… The gem, which is whispered to have been cut from the very center
> of the most perfect shard of the Moon ever found, slowly corrupted the princess, eating away her
> physical body and destroying her soul.

The fae princess who became Maab was corrupted by **Perfect Dark** — the same Phase-V black
moonstone the Anachron would need to warp time. The villainies of Arc I and the cosmology of Arc V
are the same object, seen from two ends of history.
</Oblivia>

## The blades remember

The arc's quiet marvel is that the **moonblades** — [Silverbane](/items/silverbane/), Ebonbane, Ironbane, Glassbane, and
the lost Copperbane — wake up. Away from the [Oblivia](/concepts/the-oblivia/) that erased this age, and almost reunited, the
sibling blades begin to recover memories that, for them, are simply blank spaces in an unbroken
life.

<Oblivia label="What Silverbane finally remembered">
> I remember these times much better now… much of this age was erased from history, which for you
> is written record but for me, is empty spaces spoiling my persistent memory. But now that I've
> seen myself as I was in this time — with Eldamir — I remember what it was like. Elistrae came to
> us in the body of a human girl, and the elders did not know what to do… The elvish god had fused
> to a mortal soul in order to be born and spread her grace. They had no choice in the matter.
>
> And when she fell in love with another human, but this one an avatar of a Dwarven god, a genuine
> blasphemy. Eldamir and Calix were moments away from drowning their entire landing camp in the
> Sea of Swords, but Elistrae commanded them to stand down. And then she gave them the Motherstone,
> and there was no going back.

The blades' testimony binds the whole chronicle together: the founding families — **Gallidan,
Nimue, Atlas, Carls, and Tudix** — who raised **Starfall Tower** at Izar Erori; **Peter Glans**,
the philandering king who is also the copper dragon **[Tylerjinex](/characters/tylerjinex/)**, hiding the sword **Copperbane**
in the **Penteract** (the five-dimensional ward around the **City of Dis**, "½ of Zoth's vessel");
and, behind it all, a **Masked Prophet** — an undead, impossibly old elf — who claims to have
saved the elves off the Moon before the cataclysm, and who now whispers a plan to "end the rule of
Gods and mankind in one fell swoop, send their souls into Oblivion… and restore the Moon."
</Oblivia>

## The Chronicle, and the war to come

Gathering allies — the **Horsemen** of the Dale (won by slaying **Cyric**, the God of Murder), the
witch-elves of Avalon, Brightmantle, [Calix](/characters/calix/) and Eldamir — the heroes are led by **Zebulon the
Meek**, the last prophet of **Istus** (Lady Cypher, the weaver of fate the party now serves), to
the **Chronicle**: the living archive of his forgotten people, sealed beneath a volcano the
Netherese tried and failed to breach. Zebulon carries a memory of a master, Curtis Washington, of
whom no record survives — and a vision he was given of the chronicle's end:

> I saw you, and a band of immortal heroes, step through the Deepgate at Motherstone and bow to the
> Queen of the Moon. At the same time, I watched the moon explode and form its rings, I saw the
> sinking of a ship, and I [saw] five blades piercing an opened eye. The eye had five pupils and a
> swirling iris made of eldritch runes… It bled a blood so dark that the white was obscured and
> filled with an inky void.

Inside the Chronicle, the terminal gives up the chronicle's oldest joke and its deepest truth at
once — the name of the spider-god that haunted [Motherstone](/locations/motherstone/) was never a god at all:

> "AY-ROR (error) TERMINAL LOCKDOWN COMPLETE. NO ACCESS PERMITTED TO THE CORE."

**Ayror is *error*** — the corruption in the great [moonstone](/concepts/moonstone/) minds, the same fault that broke the
[Lexicon](/items/the-lexicon/), speaking from the dawn of history.

## Into Arc VII — Leviathan

With the moonblades remembering, the founders' war against Netheril joined, and Zebulon's vision of
five blades in a single vast Eye burning ahead of them, the alliances forged in the rewritten past
converge on the present. The chronicle turns to the gathering of every force across every age, and
the rise of the thing that has been coming since the first star went dark, in
[Arc VII — Leviathan](/events/leviathan/).

The founding arc of the **Temple Holdings LLC** chronicle. A shipwreck throws a band of
strangers together as the unlikely guardians of an infant who is something far more than a
child, and sets them on a long road home across the islands of the Moonshae — pursued by an
empire, a cult, and powers older than either. The session doc opens on the world the whole
chronicle is set against:

> In this era of crumbling empires, chaos lurks just past the reach of firelight, and bides its
> time before it reconquers everything. Can the civilized races push back the Wild, or should
> they even try?

## The premise

In that age of fading order, the druidic cult of the **[Lusmundii](/factions/the-lusmundii/)** — "Light of the World" —
carries an infant in secret through hostile waters. They believe the child will turn the tide of
history against all kingdoms, laws, and cultivation, and return the world to nature. The
strangers who become her protectors are not elves and do not share her cult's faith. They are
simply the ones who happen to be aboard the wrong ship at the right moment.

## Part I — Fall of an Evening Star

The chronicle opens at Baldur's Gate, aboard a vessel of staggering scale:

> It is a great sailing ship with nearly a dozen decks. Four huge masts carrying seemingly fifty
> sails, some as big as blankets, others as big as clouds. The Evening Star, under the command
> of Captain Ysmer St. Cloud, is the flagship privateer of the Sword Coast Trading Company, the
> largest imperial charter company in the Western sea and beyond.

Her master, **Captain Ysmir St. Cloud**, is "a darkly greedy man" whose celebrated thirty-year
safe passage hides a darker trade. He is a slaver, and the ship's uncanny luck is bought with
the suffering of his **Navigator** — an enslaved dark-elf wizard kept compliant by poisoned rum,
prized for his star-magic and his **Quicksilver Sextant**, which "reveals the direction of any
known object, place or person… [and] will always avoid all storms and pirates."

The player characters board as passengers, stowaways, or prisoners. The decks teem with intrigue
— a mutiny brewing among the crew under First Mate **Hroldan Fark**; the Lusmundii rogue
**Raylin** and the disguised assassin **Delia**, each circling a mysterious prisoner; and, in the
deep hold, two secrets the Company would kill to keep. In St. Cloud's own cabin lie papers
describing a sarcophagus and "the image of a naked woman holding a sword silhouetted by the
Moon," beneath a single cryptic line: *"To keep this, you must first give it to me."*

<Oblivia label="What lay in the deep hold">
Below the cargo, in Deep Storage, St. Cloud smuggled **forty starving wood-elf slaves** and the
sarcophagus of **Daenan Gallidann** — "the well-preserved corpse of a centuries-dead elven
prince who worshipped Elistraee the Dark Maiden." Daenan is a *baelnorn*, an elven lich who
"willingly turned [himself] undead in order to stay on Earth and fight against the tide of man,"
guarding his family's forest lands until an earthquake sealed him in his tomb.

Interred with him are three relics that echo through the whole chronicle:

- **Feybane**, a dormant **Moonblade of the House of Gallidann** (+1 bastard sword, +3 vs
  Spriggans, fey and lycanthropes; attunable only by those of chaotic faith who worship
  Elistraee);
- the **Moonstone Crown**, "a circlet that once per day can emit a blinding light";
- the **Flask of Starfire**, "a flask of white light" that, with a prayer to Elistraee, "can
  bring a dying body fully back to life."

Daenan "will awaken if either his sword, crown or potion are removed."
</Oblivia>

Three plots run at once — **break out the prisoner**, **uncover the Navigator's secret**, and
**join (or stop) the mutiny** — and all three converge below decks. The arc's first chapter ends
in catastrophe.

## Part II — The Greater Storm

Ysmir St. Cloud is dead, and the Evening Star is lost with most aboard her — her riches, her
crew, and her unliving cargo sinking into the Sea of Swords. The only survivors crowd two
lifeboats kept afloat by a passenger's magic: a handful of sailors, freed slaves, the party, and
the infant.

Driven off course, they make landfall on a small, fey-touched island and step straight into an
old tragedy in miniature. The island belongs, by inheritance, to the green hag **Sycorax**, long
ago slain and bound within a lightning-struck tree; her deformed son **Calamer**; and the
air-spirit **Leira** — all of them enslaved by the exiled wizard **Fortuno**, deposed Duke of
Gorha, who rules from a tower with his daughter **Andira** and hunts the **[moonstone](/concepts/moonstone/) heart**
buried beneath the island. Readers may recognize the shape of it: the drowned-and-bound
sorceress, the enslaved air-spirit, the monstrous son, the magician-duke deposed by family and
his sheltered daughter — the Moonshae's own retelling of Shakespeare's *Tempest*.

By the chapter's end, Fortuno and Andira are dead, his tower torn apart by tempest winds, and
Sycorax is freed. In gratitude (or in payment) she opens a **Moongate** — a shimmering white
bridge arcing over the sea — and sends the survivors on to the next island.

## Part III — Snowdown

The Moongate sets them down on **[Snowdown](/locations/snowdown/)** (anciently **Wain Gallidan**), the elves' own home
island and the seat of the Cult of [Elistrae](/characters/elistrae/), the **Lusmundii**. Here the chronicle's
protagonists are named for the first time: **Drefan**, the elf sworn to [House Gallidann](/factions/house-gallidann/) who comes
to bear the moonblade **[Silverbane](/items/silverbane/)**; the wizard-merchant **Kara**; **Roland**, who hunts the
bloodline of St. Cloud; **Yoshi** and his companion **Denver**; the dwarf **Brother Magnus**; and
**Fulori**, searching for kidnapped wards of her own.

They come ashore at **Leftfork Farm**, worked by aging drow slaves under **Thaddeus Shor** and
his impossibly old father **Amadeus**. The Shors' unnatural long life is stolen: a shard of
moonstone, forged into an iron shackle, drains the life of the enslaved elven chief **Untari**
and her daughters, and a moonstone **heartstone** hidden in a nearby cave commands a crumbling
golem. To free Untari's people, the heroes fight through the goblin warren of **Heartstone Cave**
to the moonstone core itself.

## Part IV — The Good Men

Victory is soured at once. Returning to camp, the party finds the [Infanta](/concepts/the-infanta/) gone, carried off by
the wet-nurse Yoshi had trusted with her. Kara fixes the child's bearing with the Sextant, and
the chase runs west — past the burning plantation of **Grenphal** and the rescued viceroy
**Couch** — to **Bask Falls Barrow**, the ancestral tomb of House Gallidann, where the Forsworn
and their dark-fey patrons work a terrible rite. The barrow's walls are a history in stone:

<Oblivia label="What the barrow remembers — the first Infanta">
The Hall of Stories records the deep history of the Infantas. In the age the elves call **the
Heavenfall** (the **Time of Troubles**), a great cosmic mist descended and cut the gods off from
the world:

> Although a mystery at the time, it is now known that the mist was conjured by Sylvanus, the
> highest god of elves, to conceal his plans to invade the Underdark and bring his daughter Lolth
> to heel. His plan failed.

Severed from their creations, the gods warred and some fell to earth. On this island the first
**Infanta** was born: the cast-out moon-goddess **Elistrae** — innocent sister of Lolth — entered
a dying human child through its mother's prayers and grew into the living goddess-queen
**Elistrada**. Her guardian, the elf-noble **Star Quendi**, daughter of House-founder **Eldamir**,
raised her, ruled in her name, and "now sits immortal in the throne forests of **Arboria**, in
the deepest heart of the Feywild." Quendi's son **Renos**, and his son **Daenan**, kept the
island after her. Eldamir's exiled brother **Calix** refused the grave — he became a *baelnorn*,
stored fragments of his soul across the lesser isles, allied with **Queen Maab**, goddess of air
and darkness, and rose again and again to seize the island's power.

The infant the heroes are guarding is **Elistrae reborn**, and the rite at Bask Falls is an
attempt to bind Star Quendi's spirit into a mortal child so that Maab's faction can rule the
Moonshae as Elistrada once did.
</Oblivia>

The rite is broken — the returned hag **Sycorax** and the spectral cat-fiend **Greymalkin** turn
on one another over the prize — and the Infanta is recovered unharmed. The heroes learn the cost
of harming her: the **Cries of Elistrae**, a scream that "shake[s] the Earth, wracking the entire
island and bringing the walls of Harloch to the ground." Before they leave, they may commune with
the echo of [Star Quendi](/characters/star-quendi/), who blesses them and names their charge:

> Who are you, champions? Who are you, champions of Elistrae and the Moonshae Isles? Tell me your
> names so I may sing songs of you among the gildergreens in throne forests of Arboria… But she
> mustn't be raised as a god. She must be a child, living beneath the Gildergreen.

## Part V — Harloch

Carrying the Infanta back toward the cult, the party reaches the walled town of **Harloch** on a
day of festival — and into the arms of the Empire. **Commander Carlysle St. Cloud**, brother of
the captain they drowned, has arrived with a fresh garrison, claiming a great victory over the
Forsworn that the heroes know to be a lie. A letter from a dead courier reveals the deeper scheme:
Leftfork Farm and all its people have been **sold** to a buyer who means to mine its moonstone,
with the [Sword Coast Trading Company](/factions/sword-coast-trading-company/) brokering the deal. Beneath the town's derelict **Old
Temple** lies a crypt, the **Ghost of Aldawan**, and the **Gauntlet of Aldawan**; the festival
erupts into an assassination attempt, and the chapter scatters into a dozen threads as the
messengers of **Queen Maab** begin to stalk the party openly.

## Part VI — Where the Hearth Is

The garrison marches out and leaves Harloch undefended; the Jarl, twice saved by the party, begs
them to make the town their home and offers to sell them the Old Temple. Buying it, the heroes
give themselves a base, a refuge for the Cult of Elistrae — and a name. **They become "Temple
Holdings, LLC,"** the holding that gives the whole campaign its title. A new thread opens here:
**Fhastine Mageblood**, the half-elf apothecary of *The Dram*, keeps a journal she cannot read
and a moon-marked riddle of a missing mother — a clue pointing past Harloch toward the
**[Motherstone](/locations/motherstone/)**.

## Into Arc II — Motherstone

The final session splits the party. While Yoshi, Fulori, and Couch race back to shelter the
Infanta and her cult at Leftfork — now hunted by the elf-lich **[Calix](/characters/calix/)** himself — Kara and Brother
Magnus follow Fhastine's clue. A weathered dragonborn healer recognizes the moon-and-stone sigil
and leads them to a mine beneath **Snowy Mountain**, where a dead explorer left a letter for her
daughter:

> I have found its belly, but in breaking it I have undone myself… I've come so close to
> understanding. I've written it all down for you, but I fear you won't be able to read it for
> many many centuries. If you find me, please, push on and find the mother stone.

<Oblivia label="What was buried under the mountain">
Climbing a magic-bored shaft for what feels like a mile, the explorers break through a blasted
steel casing into something impossible — "a huge, metal tunnel… at least five stories high and
perfectly round," a flooded turbine hall, and at last a **Power Station**:

> Ascending from the hatch, you step up into a building so huge and ancient, but somehow far more
> advanced than any you have seen before. Blue light flows up through tiny visible conduits in the
> stone, humming from the turbines below. The light flows into man-sized pedestals throughout the
> room. On some of these pedestals, pale blue moonstones rest and glow in the energy.

Guarded by clockwork spiders, guardians, and a centurion, this buried engine — and the hunt for
the **Motherstone** the dead elf died seeking — carries directly into
[Arc II — Motherstone](/events/motherstone/).
</Oblivia>

The seventh arc is the muster before the end. A god dies, the **[Leviathan](/creatures/the-leviathan/) [Egg](/locations/the-egg/)** climbs toward the
heavens, and the whole of the world the heroes have knitted together across the ages gathers for a
single war: to tear the flying cities of **Netheril** from the sky before its queens can trigger
the **Apotheosis** that would feed all creation to a waking god.

## The Throne of Tears

The heroes escape the rising Egg in a stolen Netherese ship — the Arielle's twin, "considerably
less worn-in," rigged for Netherese spellcraft instead of St. Cloud's comforts — and limp home to
[Motherstone](/locations/motherstone/) with Percival's frozen corpse and grim news. **Brightmantle**, the God of Discovery and
Invention and **Brother Magnus's** patron, is dying; Magnus's own power fades to a guttering remnant
as his god slips away. By the time they return, Brightmantle is dead, and Motherstone weeps.

His funeral becomes the chronicle's great gathering. Every people the heroes saved across the arcs
steps forward to pledge — and to name them the rightful bearers of the sibling moonblades:

> **Atlas IV and the Sea Elves** — "You saved us from the mindflayers and defeated the Waning One.
> You are the true bearers of Glassbane. We are with you to the end."

So too the **Sun Elves** (Copperbane), the **Drow** (Onyxbane), the **High Elves** with the Weird
Sisters and Morgana (Ironbane), the **Knights of the Round Table**, the **Horsemen Clans**, the
freed people of **Memnon**, **Steve Merlin** ("You got me outta that dang tree!"), and the **Moon
Elves** with [Elistrae](/characters/elistrae/), Eldamir, and [Calix](/characters/calix/), who hail the bearer of "all the [Silverbane](/items/silverbane/)s across all
time." Last comes the copper dragon, and his pledge is the whole chronicle in one breath:

<Oblivia label="Tylerjinex's pledge — the shape of the whole chronicle">
> You traveled back in time countless times, you saved me and my entire race as a child with
> Time-Bending magic, saved the planet by moving the moon into the path of the Evening Star, left
> me with the greatest gift I'd ever received (the Tudix Cube), hunted the Traveler across the
> epochs, sacrificed yourself in multiple lifetimes, tracked me down to reignite my desire to
> destroy the greatest evil, solved my greatest dungeon, defeated my mortal enemies, and **you are
> the mother of my Egg.** I am with you to the end.

The copper dragon **Tylerjinex** — Peter Glans, husband of the dead elf-queen Alen Tudix — and
**Kara** have made an egg of their own; and he will stay behind to guard it so she can fulfill her
mission. The chronicle's cosmic stakes and its most intimate one share a single word.
</Oblivia>

Brightmantle leaves enough of himself in his final act, channeled through Magnus, that the dwarf
recovers his full power and can empower the faithful around him — the god's last gift before the
grave.

## The plan to stop the end of the world

With the armies of every age assembled, **Lex** and **Calix** lay out the final intelligence,
drawn from a captured Netherese civilian. His account is the clearest statement of the setting's
magic — and its horror.

<Oblivia label="The Weave, the Nether, and the Apotheosis of Man">
The prisoner, a demi-arcanist named **Civis**, explains how Netheril really works:

> The Weave is like a quilt, or a giant web, that all of us are a part of whether we like it or
> not. Everything flows away from it at every point in space, this is what causes time… The
> strongest of these arcanists become what you call gods… But the Mammon Machine lets us strum the
> Weave like a guitar, because unlike the Weave, the Nether sucks that energy in, causing Flux and
> Paradox.

The Empire's every wonder — its mythallar, its flying cities, its conquest of death — is powered by
**Flux**: the magic of the dead, funneled out of the natural cycle and into the **Nether** through
a network of leylines emanating from the **Obliviator** and the **Mammon Machine**. Now the
Netherese queens prepare the **Apotheosis of Man** — to bring the Mammon Machine to the **Black
Omen**, the God Egg, and "create a New Star… a moon of Pure Magic, of unlimited energy, and divine
beauty."

What they believe is salvation, Lex understands as annihilation: channel the souls of the dead into
the heart of the Egg, and **Cthulhu remerges with the remnants of Zoth, and Time and Consciousness
itself give way to total oblivion inside the mind and Will of Cthulhu.** Calix, who hears the Call
of Cthulhu in his visions, confirms the trap: the god deceived the queens through St. Cloud,
"warped their understanding so that they would bring all the magic of their world to bear against
their own existence." Civis only shrugs: *"It's foolish to think you can change nature… eventually
we are forgotten and added to the great blackness where all things reset."*
</Oblivia>

## Toward Zeal

The capital of Netheril, the flying city of **Zeal**, has begun a tremendous magical jubilee — the
culmination of faith that masks the Apotheosis. From his vantage at the peak of [Sigil](/locations/sigil/), [Tylerjinex](/characters/tylerjinex/)
reports the Old Man's House ringed in elemental storms and the **Penteract** — the five-dimensional
ward around the City of Dis, half of Zoth's vessel — "cracking and bucking at the force of some
internal pressure." The end is straining against its seams.

Three roads lie open to the assembled host: **reconnaissance**, **invasion**, and **time travel** —
the heroes' whole arsenal, pointed at last in one direction. The elves and the Earthbound races of
Toril will "tear the human cities from the sky to preserve life as we know it," and every force
across every age will converge where June foretold they must: inside the Egg, where all Times meet.

## Into Arc VIII — End of the Infanta

The war for the sky gives way to the war at the center of everything. With Brightmantle entombed,
the alliance whole, and the [Mammon Machine](/items/the-mammon-machine/) rising toward the Black Omen, the chronicle turns to its
final hour — the breaking of the Egg, the convergence of every Time, and the act of forgetting that
ends the Age of the Infanta, in [Arc VIII — End of the Infanta](/events/end-of-the-infanta/).

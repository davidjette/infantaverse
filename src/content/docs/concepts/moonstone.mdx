**Moonstone** is the substance the whole chronicle turns on — "shards of the fallen moon that store
and magnify magic, and that every faction in the Moonshae is willing to kill to control." It fell
to the world in the **[Starfall](/events/starfall/)**, the breaking of the Moon, and pooled most
densely in the Moonshae Isles and beneath [Motherstone](/locations/motherstone/).

## The phases

The Company's surveys (led by the gnome Flex Gimble) graded it by how long it lay buried out of
sunlight, growing more complex as it aged past its half-life:

- **Phase I** — a hard, glowing rock with many magical uses; "makes attractive jewelry."
- **Phase II (Power Stones)** — batteries that "release their stored energy in concentrated and
  controlled bursts," fuel for technology and the airships' Arcane Cores.
- **Phase III (Parabolic / Control Stones)** — fractal crystals "perfect for programmatically
  reflecting and intensifying arcane wave patterns"; the controllers of complex machines.
- **Phase IV (Perfect Moonstones)** — rare stones "capable of storing complex energy patterns like
  consciousness and awareness." The [Lexicon](/items/the-lexicon/) is grown in the largest of them.

<Oblivia label="Phase V — Perfect Dark">
Beyond the perfect stones is a hypothetical fifth phase the Company's researchers called **Perfect
Dark** — a moonstone "so far beyond its half life that it was twice as old as the oldest moonstone
on Faerûn," the only thing that could let the [Anachron](/items/) warp *time* as well as space. It
is not only theory: the ten-faceted black gem "cut from the very center of the most perfect shard
of the Moon ever found" is what corrupted the fae princess into **Queen Maab**. The villainy of the
first arc and the time-physics of the fifth are the same stone.
</Oblivia>

## What it really is

Moonstone is the physical form of the chronicle's deepest principle: that **mind, memory, and time**
can be held in matter. A perfect stone can mirror — or imprison — a consciousness; the
[Obliviator](/items/the-obliviator/), the Lexicon, the diadems, and the moonblades are all
moonstone instruments of thought. To mine it is to mine the stuff of souls.

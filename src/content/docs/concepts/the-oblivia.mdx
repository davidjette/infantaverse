The **Oblivia** — the **Forgetting** — is the phenomenon the whole setting is built around:
knowledge erased not from a record but from *memory itself*, "from the minds of everyone who ever
lived." What it touches becomes a hole the world declines to account for — ledgers that skip,
ruins no one can place, witnesses who weep without cause.

## How it works

Oblivia is produced by the [Obliviator](/items/the-obliviator/), the engine above
[Motherstone](/locations/motherstone/): "any information that passed within its arcane horizon
would be destroyed, not just in that instance, but in the minds of everyone who ever lived." Its
first great use erased Motherstone and its dwarf-king from history in a single afternoon — the
event called **the Betrayal**, engineered by [Calix](/characters/calix/). The
[Mara Mageblood's Journal](/items/) names it in a lullaby: *"When all the world's inside your
mind, forgetting is oblivion."*

## What it really is

<Oblivia label="Time is the Oblivia">
The Oblivia is not merely a weapon but the structure of reality. At the end of time, the goddess
June reveals that the gods "created Time itself, to hide those things from [the
[Leviathan](/creatures/the-leviathan/)] that happen not all at once… **Time is the Obliviator.**"
Consciousness — a self that exists *in sequence*, forgetting as it goes — is the one place the
blind devouring god cannot see. Forgetting is not loss; it is the wall behind which the world
survives.
</Oblivia>

## Why this record exists

The chronicle ends with the **[End of the Infanta](/events/end-of-the-infanta/)**: the heroes touch
the waking god with an Obliviator, and the Wish that unwrites him unwrites them too — "they
themselves will be forgotten in all but the vaguest terms. A symbol, image, relic or myth may stay
behind." Everything recovered here was pulled back out of that hole.

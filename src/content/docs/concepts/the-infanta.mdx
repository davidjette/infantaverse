An **Infanta** is a god reborn into a mortal child — the phenomenon that gives the chronicle's
central age its name. When the gods are shut out from the world, some "incarnate themselves in
mortal forms," and these "god-children, each and every one a human, invert the world with their
immense and terrifying sorcery and slavish followings."

## A lie at the heart of history

[Mara Mageblood's Journal](/items/) records the truth the world forgot: that the Infantas were not
deathless. "Stories always frame the Infanta as an eternal child, but she was quite the opposite.
She was a human girl who lived and died at a ripe old age." The first was
[Elistrada](/characters/), the goddess [Elistrae](/characters/elistrae/) entering "a dying human
child through its mother's prayers."

## The race for the god-children

By the [Age of the Infanta](/events/age-of-the-infanta/), as holy magic wanes and a second **Time
of Troubles** looms, the old gods are incarnating again — and every kingdom, cult, and the
[Company](/factions/sword-coast-trading-company/) races to seize them. The chronicle names many:
the **Moon** ([Elistrae](/characters/elistrae/)), **Order/Justice** (the false boy Jacob, and a
true star-charting child), **Death** ([Marcus](/characters/marcus/), the Raven Queen),
**Judgment** (Mordred), **Nature** (Sylvanus, at Myth Drannor), and the Company's profiles on
**Time, Destiny, Reason, Revelry, War, Forge, Sea, Murder, Fortune,** and **Puzzles**.

<Oblivia label="What the Infantas truly are">
Per the revelation at the end of time, the gods are "the split soul of the Star Child, whose name
was Zoth-Ommog," fragments of a divided mind hiding from their father, the blind
[Leviathan](/creatures/the-leviathan/). Each Infanta is a piece of that scattered memory, taking
mortal, sequential form — existing *in Time* — precisely so the devourer cannot find it.
</Oblivia>

The **Lexicon** is the mind of [Motherstone](/locations/motherstone/) — "a mind grown inside the
heart of the world's largest **perfect [moonstone](/concepts/moonstone/)**, a crystal able to
'stand in for the mind itself.'" It ran the city's power, water, weather, architecture, and
defenses, drawing knowledge from agents wearing **diadems** in the field and through the **Gate
Ring**.

## A library of all knowledge

The mage [Calix](/characters/calix/) opened a stable abyssal vortex to make the city a *data
vacuum*, pulling the echoes of all information in creation toward the Lexicon — "a hub of the
universe, a living library of all knowledge." But knowledge without limit drove it "cynical and
dazed," and so the [Obliviator](/items/the-obliviator/) was built to refine its mind by erasing
the world's excess detail.

## Broken

The Lexicon is broken because a single fact is missing from it: the date of **the Betrayal**, when
Motherstone went from capital of the world to wholly forgotten in an afternoon. Its broken state
even names itself — in the deep history of the [Time of Troubles](/events/time-of-troubles/), a
twin archive's terminal reports "**AY-ROR (error)** TERMINAL LOCKDOWN." The spider-god **Ayror**
that menaced Motherstone was never a god at all, but the system's own error state.

## Calix within

Calix buried his terminal-throne to *replace the Lexicon with himself* — to dissolve into the
crystal and become one with the Motherstones. The [Company](/factions/sword-coast-trading-company/)
spent years failing to "unlock the Calix puzzle," then used a stolen Palantir to override the
Lexicon and route it to an administrative key, hoping to bridge it to the
[Mammon Machine](/items/) and "broadcast… into the minds of every sentient being within a few
hundred feet of a compatible Company product." After the revolution, the insurgents recovered it,
but it remained dark — leaving [Elistrae](/characters/elistrae/) cut off from her worshippers and
her city tearing at the seams.

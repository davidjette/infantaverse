The **Obliviator** is the motionless orb suspended in the great bronze ring above
[Motherstone](/locations/motherstone/) — one of the **Infernal Machines**, "much older than human
civilization," and the source of every absence the world calls **Oblivia**.

## What it does

<Oblivia label="The nature of the Obliviator">
The Obliviator was built to refine the [Lexicon](/items/the-lexicon/), whose limitless knowledge
had grown "cynical and dazed." Its method is total:

> By its nature, any information that passed within its arcane horizon would be destroyed, not just
> in that instance, but in the minds of everyone who ever lived… Who knows what tiny wonders were
> forgotten on behalf of all by the Obliviator.

It is the engine of the **Forgetting** — the cause of the erasure of Motherstone and "the dwarf
king who built it alongside the first [Infanta](/characters/elistrae/) of the Moon." The
[Sword Coast Trading Company](/factions/sword-coast-trading-company/) could never control it, but
learned to read its leylines "to transmit magic energy and information across great distances and
even between the planes," wiring that power into every device they sell.
</Oblivia>

## Time itself

<Oblivia label="The cosmic truth — Time is the Obliviator">
At the end of time, the dying goddess June reveals what the Obliviator really is. The gods created
Time to hide their minds from the blind devourer [Cthulhu](/creatures/the-leviathan/):

> We created Time itself, to hide those things from him that happen not all at once… We are the
> memory separate from him. **Time is the Obliviator.** Our consciousness is that moment out of
> Time, the delicate world that He cannot penetrate.

The machine above Motherstone is a local instrument of a cosmic principle: forgetting is the only
wall against a god who devours by *remembering*.
</Oblivia>

## The End of the Infanta

The Obliviator is also the weapon that ends the chronicle. In [Arc VIII](/events/end-of-the-infanta/),
the heroes beat Cthulhu's head to nothing and **touch it with an Obliviator**, triggering a
shockwave of negation magic — the **End of the Infanta** — that unwrites the god, the war, and the
heroes themselves. A final Wish may choose what the world keeps, "with the consequence that they
themselves will be forgotten in all but the vaguest terms." The Oblivia is the hole this act left
behind.
